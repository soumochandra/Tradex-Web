1. What Affects Share Prices?

Stock prices are influenced by a complex interaction of demand and supply, company performance, macroeconomic factors, and market sentiment. The key drivers include:

  • Company earnings
  • Economic indicators (GDP, interest rates, inflation)
  • Market trends (bull or bear markets)
  • Global events (wars, pandemics, political changes)
  • Sector performance and peer comparisons

2. Mathematical Models Behind Share Pricing

2.1 Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)

DCF estimates the present value of expected future cash flows:

DCF = CF1 / (1+r)^1 + CF2 / (1+r)^2 + ... + CFn / (1+r)^n

2.2 Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio

P/E Ratio = Share Price / Earnings Per Share (EPS)

2.3 Moving Averages (MA)

MA(n) = (P1 + P2 + ... + Pn) / n

2.4 Bollinger Bands

Used to identify volatility and price trends:

Upper Band = MA(n) + 2*SD
Lower Band = MA(n) - 2*SD

4. Behavioral Economics in Stock Prices

Investor psychology plays a critical role in price fluctuations. Concepts like herd behavior, panic selling, and over-optimism can lead to irrational pricing. Technical patterns such as resistance and support levels are often driven by human behavior.

5. Example Calculation: Fair Value of a Stock

Assume expected cash flows of ₹100 per year for 5 years, and a discount rate of 10%.

FV = 100 / 1.1 + 100 / 1.1^2 + 100 / 1.1^3 + 100 / 1.1^4 + 100 / 1.1^5
   = ₹379.08
